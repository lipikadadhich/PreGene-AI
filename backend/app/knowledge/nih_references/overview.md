# NIH Reference Resources — Orientation

The U.S. National Institutes of Health (NIH) maintains several public
resources commonly used in clinical genetics:

- **ClinVar**: a public archive of relationships between genetic variants
  and health conditions, with variants classified as pathogenic, likely
  pathogenic, uncertain significance, likely benign, or benign, based on
  submitted clinical and research evidence.
- **GeneReviews**: expert-authored, peer-reviewed summaries of specific
  genetic conditions, covering diagnosis, management, and genetic
  counseling considerations. Each entry is written and periodically
  updated by clinical specialists in that condition.
- **MedlinePlus Genetics** (formerly Genetics Home Reference): patient
  and public-friendly explanations of genes, chromosomes, and genetic
  conditions.
- **dbSNP / dbVar**: databases cataloguing known genetic variants and
  structural variants found across sequenced populations.

## How this knowledge base should use them

This folder is intended to hold locally-saved reference material (PDFs,
exported summaries, or notes) drawn from these NIH resources for
specific diseases/genes relevant to PreGene-AI's use cases. When adding
files here, keeping the original source NIH page/report noted in the
filename or a header helps the citation engine surface accurate
attribution.
